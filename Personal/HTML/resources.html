External Websites

Restoration Team website

The Doctor Who Restoration Team is the group that remasters all Classic Who episodes for the DVD releases. Their website details their remastering process, as well as the DVD and VHS ranges.

Guide to Target novelizations

From 1973 to 1994, Target Books produced novelizations of many Doctor Who episodes. For years, this was the only way for fans to 'revisit' episodes, and today remains an excellent way to check out some of the missing episodes. This site covers every story in detail.

Doctor Who DVD FAQ

This website details information about every single release of the Doctor Who DVD and VHS range, as well as an FAQ and news about upcoming releases.

K9 official website

K9 is another Doctor Who spin-off, this time chronicling the life of K9 after leaving the Doctor. It is not a BBC show, and has no connection to Doctor Who (aside from K9 himself, and a few select, subtle references).

Big Finish Audio's Doctor Who line

Big Finish Audio's Doctor Who line is one of the few ways to get new adventures for the first eight Doctors. In addition to their main line, they also have many Doctor Who related spin-offs.

Official Tie-in Websites

Who is Doctor Who?

Seen in Rose, this is Clive's website for finding the Doctor. See Who is Doctor Who? for more information.

U.N.I.T

Seen in The Aliens of London/World War Three, this is the official website of the United Nations Intelligence Taskforce. See U.N.I.T.

Defending the Earth

After Clive (spoilers for Rose) died, Mickey took over his website, recruiting people to help him defend the Earth. Later, after Mickey (spoilers for The Age of Steel) went to Pete's World, the site was taken over by "a friend". For more information, see Defending the Earth.

Torchwood House

Seen in Tooth and Claw, Torchwood house 'opened to the public in 1981'. The site features an online observatory, as well as information about visiting Torchwood house. See Torchwood House for more.

Ghostwatch

Seen in The Army of Ghosts, this is the website for the television show Ghostwatch. This site features very little content.

Torchwood (Hi-tech) Torchwood (Lo-tech)

The Torchwood website, kept by Ianto, contains the team's case files, as well as character profiles and information about Torchwood's history.


There were once many more tie-in websites, but most have been shut down. Check out this Wikipedia article for information about out-of-commission sites.
