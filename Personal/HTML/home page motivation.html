Applying Sideways
Do well in school. Take tough classes. Interrogate your beliefs and presumptions. Pursue knowledge with dogged precision. Because it is better to be educated and intelligent than not. Be nice. This cannot be overstated. Don't be wanton or careless or cruel. Treat those around you with kindness. Help people. Contribute to your community. Pursue your passion. Find what you love, and do it. Maybe it's a sport. Maybe it's an instrument. Maybe it's research. Maybe it's being a leader in your community. Math. Baking. Napping. Hopscotch. Whatever it is, spend time on it. Immerse yourself in it. Enjoy it.

There Is No Formula
It's who you are that really matters. It's how you embrace life. It's how you treat other people. It's passion. And yes, that stuff really does drip off the page in the best of our applications. It's not anything I can explain—you just know when you read an application and a "perfect match" is there.
The whole point of life is to interact with the miracle of human consciousness on the deepest possible level. The harder you work, the luckier you get. We do what we have to do so we can do what we want to do. Think of grades/test scores as qualifying you to be considered (getting your foot in the door). Everyone has to push hard. Some have to push harder than others. If you have a small crack, you need to make a more forceful case. And sometimes, being ingenious about how you push can make all the difference. I don't always enjoy studying for classes, but I like the feeling of having studied. I like the feeling of concrete accomplishment and the notion that each good grade or resume item might help me do what I want in the future. School is a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity—to learn about the universe and its inhabitants for several hours a day at no cost to you. Being a hacker is lots of fun, but it's the kind of fun that takes lots of effort. The effort takes motivation. That's the thing about ideas—you'll never know if it's a good idea until you make it happen. To be a hacker, you have to get a basic thrill from solving problems, sharpening your skills, and exercising your intelligence. Why know the ordinary when you can understand the extraordinary? The greater the struggle, the more glorious the triumph. He who stops being better stops being good. Yield not to misfortunes, but advance all the more boldly against them. Forget past mistakes. Forget failures. Forget everything except what you're going to do now and do it. First say to yourself what you would be, then do what you have to do. Our lives are different to anybody else's. That's the exciting thing! Because nobody in the universe can do what we're doing. What's the point of being alive if you're not going to at least try to do something remarkable? If you dream big, take risks, and work extremely hard on something you're passionate about, you're in a much better position to do something great in this world.
