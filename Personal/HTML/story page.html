The Magickers

Can I do this? I ask myself. Can I really fight Callum? I grip the rusty ladder tightly. The boy in front of me, Max, scampers up to the roof with ease. Kill him, even? That thought stops me. I can’t keep—this is my job. I have to do it. I haul myself up the ladder clumsily.

The roof is blackened and burned from years of warfare. Rusty metal oil drums have that long ago lost their integrity lay everywhere, offering us little protection from the purple balls of Physsian whizzing from each side. Is this what Callum saw? I shake my head, clearing all thoughts of him from my mind. This is dangerous enough without my thoughts to distract me.

I create a shield around myself and follow Max. We army-crawl across the first building. There’s a gap between the next two—we’re forced to jump. A girl in front of me stands up to jump and gets hit by the Physsian. I wince, but we don’t stop, crawling over her body. Max makes it across easily, and it’s my turn.

I create a wall on either side, blocking me from the Physsian, and jump. I almost make it.

I grab on to the edge of the building, hitting the wall with an oomph as the wind gets knocked out of me. It’s all I can do to hang there.

Max pulls me up with ease. Odd, considering I’m a good six inches taller than him. That must be his Power; enhanced strength. I look at his stick-like arms and chuckle a little.

I follow him to the middle of the building we’re on. We must be in the middle of the compound by now. The rest of the group is circled around a hole. Max has already removed the cover.

It leads down into a big, brightly lit room. Marble, maybe. The problem is, there’s a 50-foot drop.

“I’ll go first, then!” I say. I lower myself into the hole and drop.

The group above me gasps, expecting to hear a splat. I grin a little. Callum always said I had a flare for the dramatic. Not now. I made stairs. Invisible stairs, but solid enough. I simply walk down to the floor.

It’s a big room, light steaming in through windows on every wall. Strange. There aren’t any windows on the outside of the base; must be Magick. Doorways lead to different rooms. Clumps of people—all in red jumpsuits—walk around with dark looks on their faces.

“Not much to look at, are ya,” a deep voice drawls from behind me. I whirl around, startled.

Leaning against the wall behind me is a man, perhaps in his early twenties. He has a mop of black hair, which contrasts oddly with his jumpsuit. “All covered in dirt,” he adds.

I look down. My usually pristine white shirt is covered in rust and dirt. My pants, too, are coated to the point where you can barely see the white. There’s a rip in one knee where I hit the wall. I become aware of the fact that my normally dark blonde hair is now more of a black. Soot and rust.

I chose not to reply, instead turning to watch Max make his grand entrance. He bends his knees and lands gracefully. After a moment, he turns and yells to the rest of the group, “Jump! I’ll catch you!”

One by one, they jump. I slow them down, not noticeably, but enough. Max jumps up to meet them and brings them back down carefully. Callum would have helped more, I can’t help but think. Shut up.

Once everyone is down, the man claps slowly, sarcastically. “Well done, truly…Blah blah blah. Follow me.” He walks towards one of the doors. We walk behind him, single file and silent.

He leads us into a new room, not quite as large as the first. A desk is centered against one wall. The whole thing is one big window. Outside, I can see Others, dressed in purple jumpsuits, firing at us, seemingly ineffectively.

A woman stands by the desk. Once we’re all in the room, she yells “FORMATION!”

We immediately get into formation, alphabetical order—the same way we came in—with our feet spread, hands clasped behind our backs.

“Dismissed,” she orders the man. She turns to us, coming out from behind the desk. She lectures in a clipped tone.

“Welcome to the Academy.” There is no warmth in her voice. “Here, young Magickers are trained to fight the Others.” And Callum, I mentally add. She begins pacing, staring at us. The only sound is a slight crackle coming from behind me. Someone’s Power must be electricity. “Each Magicker is born with a Power. These powers may be helped or hindered by colors.” She stops pacing. “Turn around. Dress in a jumpsuit, then get back in formation.”

Behind us, panels in the wall slide open to reveal red jumpsuits. I quickly grab one and slide it one over my clothes. A boy who I don’t recognize walks past, ‘accidentally’ shocking me. He must be the one with the electricity. “Traitor,” he mutters. They know of my brother.

I ignore him, getting back into position. The woman begins pacing again, talking faster. “As I said, colors can affect your Powers. Each color does something different. You will memorize a list, of course, but all you need to know for now is that red will block your powers,” that must be why I can’t hear the crackle anymore, “and shield you from other Magick.” She pauses. “Understand?” We nod. “Go.” She points to another door.

As we walk, the boy comes at me, shouldering me to the ground. “Spy,” he sneers, laughing.

I don’t reply, instead using my Power to push down the toe of his boot. Not enough for him to notice, certainly, but enough to make him fall flat on his face.

“Florez. Here. Now,” the woman commands. As the others leave, I stand up and walk back to her. “Octavio Florez. Younger brother to Callum Florez, believed to be the One. Currently in training with the Others.” She sits on the desk behind her, raising her eyebrow. “Care to explain?”

“Our hometown was a camp for the Others,” I explain. “The Academy took it over last year. Callum joined the Others when he came of age—I joined here.”

I open my mouth to explain the complicated mess that is our family—Yes, we both have Magick. No, I don’t know why. My Power is control over the air. Yes, that’s powerful. Callum does not have a Power. No, I don’t know why—but I never get the chance.

Behind her, the window shatters and the Others come bursting in. They grab the woman, kicking and screaming. They try to grab me too, but I throw up a shield.

“RUN!” she screams at me. I hesitate for a second—these are Callum’s warriors. His friends. Why would they hurt me?

Then I run. I am a soldier of the Academy. I run through a door, a corridor—too fast to pay attention to where I’m going.

After some time, I happen upon a classroom of sorts. I shove a desk up against the wall—the big desk, the teacher’s—and hide inside where they won’t see me.

The Others crash through the door, searching for me. They check the supply closet, the corners—but they don’t find me.

Eventually they leave the room. They leave me alone with my thoughts.

After a few hours, I can barely hear their footsteps. I go out cautiously, ready to fight them. That’s what Callum would do. No one is there. I make my way back to the room—I must have seen more than I thought—and pause outside the door.

Then I walk in, hands up. “My name is Octavio Florez,” I tell them. They immediately run at me, cuffing my arms behind my back.

I don’t protest. I sat there for hours, having a mental debate with myself. I came to a realization. They carry me out the window—I don’t fight back.

I am going to see my brother.
