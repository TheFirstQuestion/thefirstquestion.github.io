It's early in the morning.

You're sleeping peacefully in your house, dreaming of what you're going to do in the morning. You've organized a nice breakfast for Khafre, the Horus's son; he's returning home from a campaign tomorrow.

You're interrupted by a knock at the door. You shake yourself awake—this isn't the first time it's happened. But it's never a good sign. The last time a messenger woke you in the middle of the night, the Horus's mother, Hetepheres, had died.

The messenger steps through the door. He's shaking, agitated, pale with alarm, and stammering in his haste and terror. Your heart skips a beat in your chest. This can't possibly be good. And then he tells you the news: The sacred tomb of queen Hetepheres, wife of Snefru and mother of the King of Upper and Lower Egypt himself, has been entered by thieves and robbed of all its treasures.
