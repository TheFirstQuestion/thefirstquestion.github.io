This is a catastrophe.

The mighty pyramids of Dashur soar above the golden sand, but you have no eye for their splendor. The situation is worse than you had feared.

Not only had the queen's beautiful jewels been stolen, but the queen herself was missing. You had searched the nearby sand in a frenzy, but to no avail. She was long gone.

Your distress is caused not only by the loss of your former queen, but also terror of what will happen to you. As vizier, you are responsible for the royal tombs. It would have been dangerous enough to face the Horus with the fact that his mother's tomb had been robbed. It would be suicidal to tell him that her bones were stolen as well.

You can see four courses of action:
Tell the truth. You've served the Horus for years, and you know him to be a wise man. Perhaps he'll forgive you after all. Bluff. Come up with some elaborate story that keeps the Horus from learning of your mistake. Run away. Enough people owe you favors that you could last a day or two before the Horus sends his men after you. You could run to Persia and start a new life. Blame an underling. You did everything right, but the guards messed up. The Horus will have their heads, of course, but better them than you!
