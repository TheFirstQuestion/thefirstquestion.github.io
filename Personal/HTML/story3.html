You decide to bluff, and lie to the Horus.

You tell him that it was lucky his officials were so good at their jobs: not only were the thieves foiled, but you have a clever plan to prevent future danger.

With the Horus's permission, you will arrange for the queen's reburial in a new, hidden, spot, a spot so secret no one would ever find it. Of course, the move would have to be made at once, lest another attempt is made.

But you know, of course, that the king has a long day ahead of him—reports on the new canal in the Delta, visits from the treasurers, a rebellion in Nubia—so you offer to take care of the whole thing. When the tomb was sealed (at night, for security reasons), you yourself would notify the Horus so he could give his respects.

The king agreed readily. You bow and thank him. On your way out, he stops you and asks a single question:

What happened to the thieves?
You had them executed already. You've put them on trial already, and are anxiously awaiting the result. You've already had them exiled and sent far, far away to the West.
